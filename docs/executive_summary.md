# Executive Summary — Bakery Sales & Demand Analysis

**Question:** How can a bakery reduce production waste while still meeting customer demand and protecting revenue?

**Data:** A real French bakery point-of-sale export, January 2021 to September 2022 — 232,679 cleaned sales lines across 147 products, worth €562,717 in revenue.

## What the data shows

The business runs on a very small number of products. The traditional baguette alone accounts for about a third of all units sold and roughly a quarter of revenue, and a handful of products drive most of the sales. That concentration makes availability of the top items the single most important production decision.

Volume and value tell different stories. The best-selling products aren't the best-earning ones: sandwiches sell in far smaller numbers but rank near the top on revenue because of their higher price. Planning production purely on unit counts would undervalue these higher-margin items.

Demand is strongly weekend-weighted, with Sunday roughly two and a half times a typical weekday. (Wednesday shows the lowest total only because the bakery is closed most Wednesdays, not because demand is weak.) Demand also peaks sharply in July and August in both years, a repeatable seasonal pattern confirmed on a per-open-day basis. Within the day, sales concentrate in the morning, from opening to around noon.

## What it means for the business

Production should be planned around a short list of high-demand products, scaled up for weekends and the summer months, and weighted toward the morning. Higher-value items like sandwiches deserve protected availability even though their unit counts are modest. Aligning production to these patterns is the most direct route to less waste without losing sales.
