# Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis of two years of real bakery sales data. Each recommendation ties directly to a finding.

## 1. Protect availability of the top products
The traditional baguette alone is about a third of all units sold, and a small group of products drives most demand. Running out of these is the costliest kind of lost sale. Prioritise their production and never let them sell out during peak hours.

## 2. Plan by revenue, not just unit counts
Sandwiches and other higher-priced items sell in small numbers but contribute heavily to revenue. Track and protect these separately, so a units-only view doesn't lead to under-producing the most profitable lines.

## 3. Scale production to the weekly rhythm
Demand is heavily weekend-weighted, with Sunday around 2.5× a normal weekday. Build production and staffing up for Friday through Sunday, and scale back on the genuinely quiet trading days. (Wednesday's low figure reflects closures, not weak demand, so it shouldn't drive planning.)

## 4. Prepare for the summer peak
July and August are the strongest months in both years. Plan capacity, ingredient ordering, and staffing ahead of summer rather than reacting to it.

## 5. Weight output toward the morning
Sales concentrate from opening until around noon and fall away in the afternoon. Front-load baking so the morning shelves are full, and avoid over-producing for a slow afternoon that generates waste.

## Expected impact
Aligning production to these demand patterns — by product, by day, by season, and by time of day — targets the core trade-off: making enough of the right items at the right time to capture demand, while cutting the overproduction that becomes waste.

*Note: figures here come from real sales data. Waste-reduction percentages would be quantified once actual production and waste data is available (planned as a later extension using a synthetic manufacturing dataset).*
